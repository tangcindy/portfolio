Behr Summer Internship 2017

Over the summer, I had the privilege to serve as the Social Marketing Intern at Behr Process Corporation, owned by Masco Coatings Group. With a group of 8 other interns, we were presented a business challenge called the Action Learning Project (ALP) to improve the process of Generational Engagement in the Workplace.

How can we maintain a communicative workforce across all generations
and improve nondisruptive engagment in Masco Coatings Group?

The Problem

Because of Masco Coatings Group's diverse and largely spread out employee population in terms of geography and generational gaps, there is a lack of connection between employees, employees and their managers, and employees with the company. According to the Workplace Dynamics Engagement Survey, the Vital 3 aspects of appreciation, meaningfulness, and open-mindedness need to be addressed more to improve employee retention and loyalty.

Tools Used

User interiews, Observations, Personas, Sketch, Adobe Illustrator

Research Findings & Personas

After identifying the overall population at Masco Coatings Group, we segmented the employees into categories according to their generation. The generations we focused on were Baby Boomers, Gen X, and Millennials, who all have different goals and behaviors in the workplace.

User Research
  1. How does your department allow you to express appreciation, open-mindedness, or meaningfulness to others?
  2. What does engagement mean to you?
  3. What makes you feel like your work is meaningful?
  4. How do you feel about the generational gap in the workplace?

In order for us to analyze, understand, and humanize our research data, we created three seperate personas for employees of each generation. Leadership and management styles, career goals, and employee satisfaction turned out to be extremely diverse in each department. 74% of employees had different access to resources and only 45% were exposed to individual recognition initatives available to the department.

Generating Solutions

Revamping existing initiatives

We found that the two most vital moments for employees to feel connected with their company are when they are first hired and the opportunities they have to engage with each other while growing. Thus, we modeled two solutions that were actually integrated apart of our own intern class and applicable to the entire Masco Coatings Company.

Experience Exchange Program

The Experience Exchange Program will consists of a 10-week program, allowing particpants to explore career paths and emersion into Behr's culture. It is a combination of a mentoring and reverse-mentoring model, targeting new-hires, entry level employees, and interns. Participants on both ends will fill out this survey created by Typeform to gauge interests for the program.


Masco Coatings Game

Our goal here with the games is to continue strengthening engagement across the departments through task completion and friendly competition. Out of all the users we spoke with, only several departments actually get to interact with each other on a daily basis according to production. In order to create and facilitate opportunities of engagement, we created an application prototype for users to submit points for the games with quarterly incentives to participate.

Conclusions

Essentially, the added values of these two programs are significant for Masco Coatings Group. There are opportunities for further growth, suchs as spin-off programs like PR, community relations, and marketing campaigns from the initiatives as well as cost savings in wellness programs implemented through the Masco Coatings Games. The people benefits are tied to Behr's Guiding Principles and will increase Key Performance Indicators through survey results increasing a goal of 5%. I had a great time getting to understand how Behr's culture came to be and how I could find the niche areas to improve gaps between generations!
